What are one-on-one meetings?

One-on-one meetings in Zoho Bookings help two people find the right time to meet each other. You showcase your availability, and the customer books an appointment with you in seconds.

How Zoho Bookings helps schedule one-on-one meetings

Zoho Bookings is a versatile scheduling app, allowing students to self-schedule appointments in seconds and saving you hours of manual work. This means you can focus on teaching your sessions-rather than scheduling them, notifying students and collecting payments-all using a single app.

Choose a workspace

Workspaces are virtual spaces for a set of services. For instance, if you run mental health clinics across the state using multiple workspaces, you’d choose your preferred workspace and name your meeting. You can stick to the default workspace if you operate your business from a single location.

Set your service duration

Determine the length of your one-on-one meetings, whether it’s minutes or hours long. For example, if your company uses Zoho Bookings for internal meetings, create multiple one-on-ones with different durations to suit your preferences.

Pricing your services

Mark appointments as free or paid based on your requirements. For instance, as a private tutor with multiple one-on-ones, you can charge for each service as you see fit. You can also collect deposits to avoid no-shows.

Enhance one-on-ones with integrations

Host virtual meetings and remote sessions by integrating with other Zoho apps. If you work in customer support or sales, integrate with meeting tools like Zoho Meeting, Zoom, Google, Teams, or remote support software such as Zoho Assist for seamless customer facing sessions.

Assigning staff to meetings

Allocate one or multiple staff members to your one-on-one meetings. When a customer books with you, appointments are allocated in a round-robin fashion to ensure equal staff utilization.
